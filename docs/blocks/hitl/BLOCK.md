## Overview

HITL is the control plane that keeps a human in the loop over an autonomous AI agent. It owns three surfaces: **approvals** (`services/approval/`) — a pending-action queue where risky agent actions wait for operator sign-off, plus an SMS command processor that lets operators approve/deny by text; the **autonomy mandate** (`services/autonomy_mandate.py`) — the per-workspace policy that defines what the agent may do unattended versus what must escalate; and **nudges** (`services/nudges/`) — proactive, generated prompts that surface "you should act on this" moments to operators, driven by a library of pluggable strategies (hot lead, deal stall, no-show recovery, birthday/anniversary, approvals waiting, unresponsive, referral ask, and more). The `approval_worker` drains and delivers approvals, the `nudge_worker` generates and delivers nudges, and `autonomy_health` monitors mandate health.

**This HITL pattern is reusable across any AI-agent product, not just CRM.** An approval gate, an autonomy/escalation policy, and a strategy-driven nudge generator are domain-agnostic primitives — the CRM-specific parts are confined to the executors the gate calls and the strategies' triggers. Extracted behind ports, this block is the "supervise an agent" substrate for any vertical.

## How to Extract

1. Pull `core` plus `agent-brain`, `appointments`, `contacts`, `messaging`, and `voice` transitively. Because agent-brain and automations import the approval gate and autonomy mandate back, extract HITL together with agent-brain or expose the gate/mandate over an interface to break the cycle.
2. Copy `owns_paths` (approval/nudges services, autonomy_mandate, two routers, three workers, three model files, pending-actions + nudges frontend).
3. Sever sideways imports by injecting executor ports: an outbound-delivery + SMS port (messaging/voice), a booking port (appointments), a contact-resolution port (contacts), and an `approved_action_executor` lookup port (agent-brain). The generic gate/queue/strategy machinery stays; only the concrete executors are repointed.
4. Mount the two routers; export `approval_gate_service`, `command_processor_service`, and the `autonomy_mandate` helpers for the blocks that gate through them.
5. No block-specific env vars — relies on core boot vars and the SMS transport's credentials (owned by voice).
6. Port the three tables (`pending_actions`, `human_nudges`, `human_profiles`) and their creating revisions.
7. Register `approval_worker`, `nudge_worker`, and `autonomy_health` in the new runner gated on `RUN_BACKGROUND_WORKERS`.

## Risks

- **Bidirectional coupling with agent-brain:** the gate calls agent-brain to execute approved actions while agent-brain calls the gate to enqueue them — split them together or behind a registry.
- **Autonomy policy fan-out:** `autonomy_mandate` (including `BATCH_PACKS`) is read by messaging's `message_trace` and agent-brain's payment tools; if it doesn't travel with them, escalation decisions silently change.
- **Action execution safety:** the approval gate reaches into messaging/appointments/voice/contacts to perform real-world actions; an incomplete executor port can either no-op approvals or fire them without the intended guardrails.
- **SMS command trust boundary:** `command_processor_service` lets operators drive approvals over inbound SMS — preserve the sender-verification/trust checks when repointing the transport.
- **Strategy drift:** the nudge strategy registry assumes the domain models it inspects (contacts, deals, appointments) exist; pulling nudges without those blocks leaves strategies that never fire.
